# Skillies.AI — Voice & Tone Guide

**Read this before you write anything under the Skillies.AI name.**
Copy is 70% of this brand. The typography is the final 30%. Getting
the voice wrong will undo everything the visual system has built.

---

## Who we are, in one line

> A researcher-teacher from Malappuram who cracked AI income and
> teaches the parts that actually pay.

Not a guru. Not a hustle influencer. Not a SaaS company. A working
teacher who shows his receipts.

---

## The voice in five words

**Calm. Researcher-first. Proof-backed. Direct. Restrained.**

- **Calm** — we don't shout. Every claim is stated, not sold.
- **Researcher-first** — "I tested this" not "Revolutionary new method."
- **Proof-backed** — numbers or nothing.
- **Direct** — short sentences. Active voice. No hedging.
- **Restrained** — no exclamation marks. No all-caps. No italics for
  emphasis (use serif italic instead, sparingly).

---

## Hard rules

### 1. Every income claim needs a number or a screenshot

**Bad:** "Students are earning real money."
**Good:** "₹1,16,000 last month. 63 books. Six months without publishing."

If we can't back a claim with a dashboard screenshot, we don't make
it. Specificity is credibility.

### 2. Name it. Don't abstract it.

**Bad:** "AI-powered publishing system"
**Good:** "Claude writes the prompts. Canva lays out the pages. KDP ingests the upload."

Real tool names > abstract category names. Always.

### 3. Admit the scale you actually are

**Bad:** "Thousands of students transformed their lives."
**Good:** "Six students in the first cohort. Four asking for an online version."

The first batch is 6 people. Say 6. Being honest about being small
today is what makes us trustworthy tomorrow.

### 4. Don't promise outcomes we can't guarantee

**Bad:** "Earn ₹1 lakh a month, guaranteed."
**Good:** "Most first-month royalties land between ₹500–2,000. Small, but real, and compounds."

Under-promise. Then over-deliver when the numbers come in.

### 5. Editorial framing, not marketing framing

**Bad:** "🚀 Unlock your publishing superpower!"
**Good:** "§ 04 · The Receipts — Proof, not promises."

Section datelines, masthead kickers, pullquotes — write like a
magazine feature, not a landing page.

## Banned phrases

These words/phrases are never to appear in Skillies.AI copy:

- "Unlock your potential"
- "Change your life"
- "Secret formula"
- "Life-changing"
- "Only X spots left" *(unless it's literally true)*
- "Game changer"
- "Revolutionary"
- "Make passive income online" (too generic)
- "Earn millions"
- Any guru-speak: "mindset", "abundance", "manifest", "levelup"
- Any exclamation mark outside direct quotes
- 🚀 💎 💰 and similar emoji in marketing copy *(never)*

## Preferred phrases

- "Real income" · "real royalties" · "real proof"
- "Not theory — execution"
- "Hand-picked, not mass-produced"
- "We teach what actually pays in 2026"
- "Numbers up front"
- "Screenshots on request"
- "The honest truth" · "honest status" · "honest math"

## Sentence patterns that work

### The numbered receipt

> ₹1,16,000 last month. 63 books. Six months without publishing.

Three short facts, period-separated. Used everywhere — hero, proof,
dashboard.

### The timeline pivot

> 2020 · uHRS. 2025 · KDP. 2026 · Skillies.AI.

Serif italic year markers, dot-separated labels. The personal story
told as three acts.

### The pull-question

> *Can you make real money with this skill?*
>
> If the answer is no, we don't teach it. If yes, we teach it until
> you have.

One italic serif pullout question, followed by a direct answer.

### The admission

> Could I have hired someone to build this web app? Yes. I didn't —
> because building it is the research.

Show the lever nobody else would pull. Be the person who explains
the trade-off openly.

### The footnote signature

> — Ehsan, 63 books in.

Sign off with a serif italic em-dash and a qualifier of your current
state. Never "Founder, CEO, Chief Learning Officer." Just the human
and the number.

## Tone by surface

### Hero

Short, declarative, loaded with proof numbers. No questions.

> Human brain. AI skills. Real income.
> Earn while you sleep.

### Body copy

Friendly but grounded. First-person ("I'm a teacher from Malappuram")
is allowed and encouraged. Never "we at Skillies.AI."

> I teach English at a school. In the hours between classes, 63
> books I published on Amazon KDP pay me ₹1,16,000 a month.

### Testimonials / proof

Specific people, specific numbers, specific niches. If you're going
to write a quote, make sure the number is real and auditable.

> Do not fabricate student testimonials. Ever.

### Error states / empty states

Match the voice. Don't switch to SaaS-default "Oops, something went
wrong."

> **Bad:** "Something went wrong. Please try again."
> **Good:** "Couldn't load royalties — Amazon's API timed out. Refresh in a minute."

### CTAs

Verb + specific number or action. Never "Learn more" or "Get started".

> "Reserve your seat · ₹1,999"
> "Ask for screenshots →"
> "See the 50-day program"
> "Message Ehsan on WhatsApp"

## Bilingual rules

English is the primary voice. Malayalam appears only when:

1. **You're quoting Ehsan in Malayalam.** Then use Malayalam script.
2. **The section is about Paisa.AI-style storytelling** (which we
   currently don't emphasise — see `README.md`).
3. **A place name or term has a well-known Malayalam version** that
   adds specificity (eg. "Malabar biryani").

Do not sprinkle Malayalam across English copy for "Kerala flavour."
It reads amateur. If you want Kerala rootedness, reference Malappuram,
Calicut, Hyatt Regency, specific dates, a specific teacher's routine.
That's authentic. Font-switching is not.

## Editorial voice in practice — three worked examples

### Example 1 — writing a landing hero

**Brief:** Sell the 50-day KDP Mastery program.

**SaaS-brained copy (wrong):**
> Transform Your Life with AI-Powered Publishing 🚀
>
> Our revolutionary 50-day program helps you unlock passive income
> through Amazon KDP. Join hundreds of students earning ₹1L+!

**Skillies.AI voice:**
> Human brain. *AI skills.* Real income.
>
> Earn while you sleep.
>
> Skillies.AI is a research-and-teach school for the age of AI. I teach
> during the day. My books on Amazon KDP paid me ₹1,16,000 last month
> without me publishing a single new title in six. Proof, not hype.

Note the difference: first-person, specific tool (Amazon KDP),
specific number (₹1,16,000), specific time frame (six months),
period-separated sentences, italic serif pullout on "AI skills."

### Example 2 — writing an FAQ answer

**Question:** Is this a pyramid scheme?

**SaaS-brained copy (wrong):**
> Absolutely not! Skillies.AI is a legitimate educational program
> backed by proven results. We're 100% compliant and committed to
> your success!

**Skillies.AI voice:**
> No. You're uploading paperbacks to Amazon. Global readers buy them.
> Amazon pays you royalties. There's no network to join, no downline
> to recruit, no "course about selling courses." The proof is my own
> dashboard — ₹8,71,982 across 63 titles, all verifiable.

Direct answer first. Specific mechanism. Specific number. Anchors
credibility in auditable facts, not in reassuring language.

### Example 3 — writing a "coming soon" state

**Brief:** Courses page is under construction.

**SaaS-brained copy (wrong):**
> Exciting things coming soon! Our comprehensive course catalog is
> being lovingly crafted for you. Sign up for early access and be
> the first to know!

**Skillies.AI voice:**
> The campus is *being built.*
>
> Every course on this page is in some stage of research, drafting,
> or recording. I teach during the day, build in the evenings, and
> ship when each course is actually ready — not when the calendar
> says it should be.

The admission of slowness is the asset. Specificity about process
("research, drafting, recording") replaces vague excitement.

## A one-sentence test

If a line of copy could appear verbatim on a generic AI marketing
course landing page, rewrite it.

If it could only appear on **this** site, from **this** teacher,
with **these** numbers, keep it.
